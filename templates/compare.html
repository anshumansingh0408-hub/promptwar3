🌿 India's Climate Commitments & Net Zero Pathway

Paris Agreement Targets

Under its Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs), India has committed to reducing the emissions intensity of its GDP by 45% by 2030 (from 2005 levels) and achieving 50% cumulative electric power installed capacity from non-fossil fuel sources by 2030.

Path to Net Zero by 2070

Announced at COP26, India's target is to reach Net Zero emissions by 2070. This requires a rapid scale-up of renewable solar energy, transitioning railways to 100% electrification, establishing green hydrogen corridors, and adding carbon sinks via forestation.

Individual Action: LiFE

The Government of India's "LiFE" (Lifestyle for Environment) movement emphasizes that individual lifestyle changes—such as reducing food waste, conserving water, preferring public transit, and adopting circular economy habits—play an indispensable role in climate action.
